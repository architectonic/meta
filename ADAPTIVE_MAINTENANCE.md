# Adaptive Maintenance

## Review loop

```text
observe outcomes and failures
-> identify the smallest material weakness
-> locate its primary layer
-> propose a bounded change
-> verify against representative work
-> record evidence and authority
-> keep, revise, or retire
```

## What to measure

- time and tool calls to locate canonical context;
- unsupported assumptions treated as facts;
- authority or privacy mistakes;
- failed handoffs and repeated rediscovery;
- stale or contradictory knowledge;
- loop cost, value, failure, and review burden;
- unused, duplicate, or obsolete artifacts.

## Guardrails

- Do not add documents merely to demonstrate maintenance activity.
- Do not allow self-audit to become self-approval.
- Do not preserve a profile, loop, graph, or skill after its value disappears.
- Derived indexes and graphs may be rebuilt; canonical history and decisions require governed change.
- Material changes name the human or delegated authority that approved them.
